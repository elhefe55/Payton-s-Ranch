The "Resume Builder" Strategy

To get a high-paying ranch management job later, you need a resume that proves you can handle the grit:

  1. Volunteer First: Offer 4 hours a weekend at a local barn in exchange for "ground work" lessons.
  2. The Summer Internship: Use your college summers (leveraging TIP) to apply for wrangler jobs via CoolWorks.
  3. Join the Bureau: Network with the Michigan Farm Bureau's "Young Farmers" to find land-owning mentors.
