Start Where You Are

You don't need to own 100 acres to start your ranching career. In Michigan and across the country, there are entry-level positions designed for people exactly like you: hardworking, willing to learn, and passionate about horses.

Skills You Need to Build Now

Ranching isn't just riding; it's land management, mechanics, and livestock health. Focus on collecting these "skills" like badges.

Tractor Operation Fence Repair Basic Equine First Aid Truck & Trailer Driving Pasture Management Hay Management

The "Resume Builder" Strategy

To get a high-paying ranch management job later, you need a resume that proves you can handle the grit. Here is your 3-step approach:

  1. Volunteer First: If you have zero experience, offer to work 4 hours a weekend at a local barn in exchange for "ground work" lessons.
  2. The Summer Internship: During your first year of college (using TIP), apply for a summer wrangler job via CoolWorks.
  3. Join the Bureau: Join the Michigan Farm Bureau's "Young Farmers" group to find mentors who actually own land in Michigan.
